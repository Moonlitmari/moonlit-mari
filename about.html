Stardust Picks

Who I am, what I love, and everything in between. My watchlist, cosplan wishlist, the games I play at 2am, and the random details that make me me. No agenda, no algorithm — just a girl who loves too many things and wants to share all of them. Drop a suggestion at the bottom!

7+ Years cosplaying

The maker behind the magic

Hello, I'm Mari

I'm a self-taught cosplayer, former theatre kid, con-going dreamer, and beginner/intermediate cosplayer who stumbled into this craft and never looked back. I'm not a professional. I make mistakes on camera and off. I hot-glue my fingers, prick myself with the needles, and always seem to be reaching toward a seam ripper more than once during every project.

I want this space to be for anyone who's ever looked at a cosplay and thought "I could never do that." Quit lying to yourself, because you totally can. You just need a starting point and a crew that roots for you. Consider yourself a part of the crew, stardust! We are here to cheer you on every step of the journey.

When I'm not sewing, you'll find me at conventions, running panels, playing cozy games at midnight, or taste-testing new teas while watching the moon through my studio window.

The random details

A little more about me

No cosplay content here. Just the things that make me me — food opinions held with great conviction, color feelings, and the general chaos of being Mari.

I am a soup person

Despite what some people apparently believe, I am not a picky eater — I just have very strong opinions. And my strongest opinion is that soup is one of the greatest foods on this earth. Sopa de Pollo at every Mexican restaurant, ramen, hot pot, Thai coconut shrimp soup, corn soup, Zuppa Toscana... I am a soup wench and I have the shirt to prove it.


Outside of soup: salmon salad, cajun shrimp tacos, classic street tacos with those crispy fresh onions, and an excellent chicken tender. Taco Bell saw me through high school and college and I will not abandon it now. Also Pita Mediterranean's chicken shawarma is genuinely life-changing and the greek fries are the best fries.

My snack lineup

If you come to my studio you might find: popcorn with extra butter, Chocolate Churro Turtle Chips (if you know you know and if you don't please find them immediately), feta dip with Ritz Thins, chocolate covered peanuts, or the little unwrapped Kit Kat snack bags because I love them specifically in that format and I will not explain why.


Also pickles. Kimchi. Pickled radish. I am a fermented foods advocate and I stand firmly by this position.

My favorite color & style

Lilac purple — which, if you have looked at literally any part of this website, will come as absolutely zero surprise. But truly I love all pastels and bright colors. If it is cheerful and soft I am probably already interested.


My style has evolved a lot. I used to be very preppy and vintage coded. Now I live in lounge wear, a good pair of jeans, and graphic tees. Theatre kid to cozy gremlin is a completely natural evolution and I have fully embraced it.

Theatre kid to cosplayer

Every costume I make is still, at its core, a performance. I never actually stopped being a theatre kid — I just found a different stage. The convention floor is my stage now and honestly? The cosplay audience is just as enthusiastic as any theatre crowd and significantly more likely to quote Star Wars at me, which I consider a genuine upgrade.


If you were also a theatre kid: hi, welcome, you are going to love it here. Pull up a chair. The sewing machine is already warm.

You asked, I answered

Frequently asked things

Are you self-taught?

90%! No formal training — just YouTube rabbit holes, library books, and a lot of ruined fabric. Everything I know I learned by doing (and undoing), but my mom did teach me how to read patterns and use the sewing machine.

How long does a costume take?

Depends on the build and the time I can dedicate to it. If I really put my mind to it I can finish most costumes in 2–3 months, but I have been known to finish an entire build 2 days before con. (Peep the Maomao cosplay!)

Do you take commissions?

Not at this time! I make everything for myself and for the love of the craft. Maybe one day — but for now I'm just here to share the journey. I am always happy to help you brainstorm and jumpstart your journey though.

What's your setup like?

A dedicated craft room with a cutting table, a sewing machine and serger, 6 hot glue guns, fabric hordes in the corner, and scraps saved for later. Plus a designated spot for the cats to sleep without being on the fabric.

How can I support you?

Subscribe on YouTube, follow along on Instagram and TikTok, or just share my content with someone who loves cosplay. That means the world to me.

Book series spotlight

The Padme Amidala Trilogy by E.K. Johnston

Queen's Peril. Queen's Shadow. Queen's Hope. Three books that follow Padme from her time as Queen of Naboo through her years in the Senate during the Clone Wars — with her handmaidens, her political courage, and her quiet heartbreak all front and center. This trilogy gave Padme Amidala the story she deserved and I think about it constantly. E.K. Johnston understood the assignment completely. If you are a Star Wars fan who has not read these — please. I am asking. Go read them.
