The maker

Hello, I'm Mari

I'm a self-taught cosplayer, seamstress, and chronic dreamer based in [your city]. What started as duct tape and cardboard in my childhood bedroom has grown into a full studio practice — and a floor that is perpetually covered in foam scraps.

I make costumes that feel like magic — fantasy armor, flowing gowns, creature builds, wigs styled to impossible heights. Every piece is made by hand with an embarrassing amount of glitter and a very patient hot glue gun.

When I'm not sewing, you'll find me at conventions, running panels on cosplay craftsmanship, or taste-testing new teas at midnight while watching the moon through my studio window.

The origin story

How it all began

2017

First convention

Walked into my first anime convention in a store-bought costume and immediately knew I needed to make my own. Bought a glue gun that same week.

2018

First handmade build

Completed my first fully handmade costume — foam armor held together with prayer and contact cement. Won a beginner's craftsmanship award. Cried happy tears.

2020

Started sharing online

Pandemic gave me time to document my process. Posted my first tutorial. The response was overwhelming and deeply humbling.

2022

First convention panel

Was invited to run my first panel on beginner foam armor. Shook for the entire hour. The room was full and asked the best questions.

2024

Moonlit Mari is born

Launched this site, the community, and officially became a full-time cosplay creator. The dream is very much alive under the moonlight.

You asked, I answered

Frequently asked things

Are you self-taught?

100%! No formal training — just YouTube rabbit holes, library books, and a lot of ruined fabric. Everything I know I learned by doing (and undoing).

How long does a costume take?

Simple builds: 2–3 weeks. Complex armor: 2–4 months. That enormous dragon build from last year? Seven months and approximately zero sleep.

Do you take commissions?

Not at this time! I make everything for myself and for the love of the craft. Maybe one day — but for now I'm just here to share the journey.

What's your setup like?

A dedicated craft room with a cutting table, two sewing machines, a heat gun station, and a shelf of reference art that I may have a problem with.

Can I use your tutorials commercially?

Personal use is always free. For commercial use of my patterns or techniques, please reach out via the contact page to discuss licensing.

How can I support you?

Subscribe on YouTube, follow along on Instagram and TikTok, or just share my content with someone who loves cosplay. That means the world to me.
